BOOKING RULES

  • Only a member can make a booking. The booking secretary may at their discretion refuse to accept a booking by a member who owes money to the Club.
  • The conduct of each visitor is the responsibility of the member who booked for that visitor, irrespective of whether or not the member accompanies the visitor. It is also the member's responsibility to ensure that all jobs allocated to each bedroom occupied by the member or visitors booked in by the member are done properly.
  • No member or visitor may stay at the lodge without a booking. Any person who is at the lodge and wishes to extend their stay may do so only by the member who booked for that person obtaining the approval of the booking secretary.
  • Bookings by mail are open immediately by writing to Peter Lewinsky at 59 Bayview Crescent, Black Rock 3193- email skimitre@yahoo.com.au. Please use the booking form to provide all relevant information. A cheque for the full amount of the lodge fees for the bookings must be enclosed with bookings made by mail.
  • Phone bookings open after the Annual General Meeting. The phone booking hours are 7.30pm to 9.00pm on Mondays and Thursdays only (Booking Nights) to Peter Lewinsky on 0417 325 650.
  • Bookings by phone and e-mail will only be processed on Booking Nights (although e-mails can be sent to Peter at any time). Payment must be received by Peter by the next Booking Night.
  • All bookings received on or before Friday 30 April shall be regarded as having been received on 30 April. The committee may conduct a ballot to determine the priority of such bookings. Otherwise, the priority of bookings made in accordance with Rules 4 and 5 will be the order of receipt by the booking secretary who may conduct a ballot or ballots to determine the priority of such bookings received on the same day. Priority of phone bookings will be lost if payment in full is not made within 3 business days.
  • If a booking is cancelled 8 days or more in advance, any lodge fees paid for that booking will be refunded. Otherwise, payment in full must be made regardless of attendance, and no refunds will be allowed other than at the discretion of the booking secretary if there is no snow.
  • No booking will be accepted prior to 1 June for:
    • an unaccompanied visitor, or
    • more than 3 accompanied visitors to stay at the lodge -
      • for a weekend during the ski season,
      • during the school holidays, or
      • during race week.
  • There is no restriction on bookings made:
    • on or after 1 June, or
    • for any mid week period other than the school holidays,
    • or race week.
  • Weekend bookings must be for both Friday and Saturday nights.
  • A child under 4 years of age must not stay at the lodge other than during the family period.
  • A person under 18 years of age must not stay at the lodge unaccompanied by a parent or guardian without the prior approval of the Committee. Any application for approval should be made through the booking secretary in sufficient time prior to the intended stay for the Committee to properly consider it.
  • A person must not be booked to stay at the lodge, or actually stay at the lodge, for more than 20 nights during the ski season without the prior approval of the Committee.
  • For the purposes of these rules:
    • the school holidays are from Sunday night, 27 June to Sunday night, 11 July and from Sunday night, 19 September to Sunday night, 3 October;
    • the family periods are from Sunday night, 19 September to Sunday night, 3 October and from Sunday night, 25 July to Thursday night, 29 July; and
    • race week is from Sunday night, 22 August to Thursday night, 26 August.
  • Each person staying at the lodge must use only the bedroom designated for their use by the booking secretary.
  • The Winter Managers may at their discretion and in consultation with the booking secretary accept visitors to occupy empty bedrooms.
  • The lodge fees per night during the season are:
    • Committee member: $10
    • First accompanied visitor of committee member: $10
    • Club member: $15
    • Family visitor of club member (spouse or child): $20
    • First accompanied visitor of club member where no family visitor booked: $20
    • Second or subsequent visitor mid-week (including mid-week school holidays): $50
    • Second or subsequent visitor weekend: $70
    • Visitors organised by the Winter Manager: $85.
  • The lodge fees for off season use per night are:
    • Club member: $10
    • Family visitor of club member (spouse or child): $10
    • First accompanied visitor where no family member booked: $10
    • Any other visitor: $15
    • Where the member wants exclusive use of the lodge a minimum fee of $250 shall apply.

BOOKING INFORMATION

Lodge fees remain the same as last season for members and their families or first accompanied visitors. However, the Committee felt it was appropriate to increase the fees for second or subsequent visitors by $5 and for visitors organised by the Winter Manager by $10. Accordingly, the fees for a second or subsequent visitors mid week will be $50, on weekends will be $70 and for Winter Manager visitor will be $85. It is hoped that these increases will not impact significantly on members and their families but will give the Club's finances a small boost as we look to reduce our debt over the next couple of years.

Bookings by mail and email are now open. Telephone bookings are NOT open yet, except for off-season use.

This year the Booking Secretary role will be carried out by Peter Lewinsky (subject to formal election at the AGM). All bookings should be directed to Peter. His address, phone number and email address are on the booking form. The Booking Secretary's role is extremely time consuming. Please try and minimise the burden by sticking to the Rules.

As always, if a booking is not followed by prompt payment, the booking will be automatically lost. It is your responsibility to ensure that your booking and your guests bookings are paid for on time.

At this stage, the easiest method is mailing a completed booking form to Peter together with a cheque for the lodge fees.

The booking priority period for members is until (and including) Friday 7 May. If, at that date, any nights are over-booked, positions will be balloted. I encourage you to submit your bookings during the priority period to avoid disappointment.

Telephone booking night will again be on Monday and Thursday between the hours of 7.30pm and 9.00pm. Please observe these hours. You can send emails at any time (which the Committee encourages). The email will be checked at least on, every Monday and Thursday so you should assume that your email will not be received until the Monday or Thursday after you send it. Bookings must be paid for in full by the booking night after that (for example, if you send an email to Peter on Sunday, he will receive it on Monday and your booking will be held until Thursday).

If you want to book for more than 20 nights, please let Peter so that he can seek the prior approval of the Committee in accordance with Rule 13.
